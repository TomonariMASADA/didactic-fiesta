> We call the fixed point x satisfying x = pro(re(an(x))) Horizont or bestimmte Unbestimmtheit. ([126](126.md))

What we call Horizont here is what is called ‘determinants of definiteness’ by Ivor Leclerc in the following passage, which is quoted by Debaise, Didier. *Speculative Empiricism : Revisiting Whitehead*, Edinburgh University Press, 2017 on p.93.

> thus in so far as eternal objects enter into this prehension, it is not as themselves data, but only as determinants of the definiteness of the data

Then what we call Sinn in [126](126.md) is the determinants of the definiteness of the future.

And what we call Ereignis in [126](126.md) is the determinants of the definiteness of the past.

The data is what is given. The future is what will be given. The past is what was given.
